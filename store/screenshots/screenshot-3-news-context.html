Fed's Tightening Policy Rattles Markets as Inflation Persists
By Sarah Chen · Feb 25, 2026 · 8 min read
The Federal Reserve's quantitative tightening measures have precipitated a contraction in liquidity, exacerbating inflationary pressures amid supply chain disruptions. Analysts warn that the ripple effects may extend into the second quarter as borrowing costs remain elevated.
Market participants are closely monitoring yield curve dynamics, with some economists forecasting a potential inversion that historically correlates with recessionary periods. The central bank's dual mandate of price stability and maximum employment continues to create policy tensions.
