Photosynthesis
From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Photosynthesis is a biological process used by photoautotrophs to convert light energy, normally from the sun, into chemical energy that can be stored and later released to fuel the organism's activities. It is one of the most important biological processes on Earth, producing the oxygen that most organisms require to survive.
The overall equation for photosynthesis is deceptively simple, but the underlying biochemical pathways are remarkably complex. The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes, while the light-independent Calvin cycle reactions take place in the stroma of the chloroplast.
